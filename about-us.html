About Myself

When I started university at University of Piraeus in the fall of 2015, I had my sights set on becoming a digital marketing manager and working in the business technology. I chose to study Computer Science within the Digital Systems Department in order to gain the development, design, and creativity skills necessary to pursue a career as a digital marketing manager and front-end developer. During my three years at Piraeus, I've excelled in all of my developing, design and computer science classes, and I've learned a lot about management, communication, and leadership through my extracurricular activities. Over the last year, I've become increasingly interested in nonprofit work. I believe that I can use the marketing and communication skills I've gained at university to help nonprofits with their communications, public relations, management, and grant writing. Since September 2017, I have been serving as an B2C member for AIESEC Greece in Piraeus. I hope to gain ample experience in nonprofit work in my own community before pursuing a master's degree in digital marketing. My experiences as a front-end developer, B2C member, teacher, and learner have taught me how to be a prepared and effective communicator in an extent level.
